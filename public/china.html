Unveil China

A Nation of Timeless Traditions and Cutting-Edge Innovation

Great Wall of China

"One of the most iconic landmarks in the world, the Great Wall of China stretches over 13,000 miles, weaving through mountains, valleys, and deserts. This ancient fortification, built to protect against invasions, offers spectacular views and a glimpse into China's rich history and engineering marvels."

Forbidden City

"Located in the heart of Beijing, the Forbidden City was once the imperial palace of the Ming and Qing Dynasties. With its vast courtyards, halls, and intricate architecture, it now serves as a museum showcasing China's royal heritage."

Li River

"Famous for its karst mountain landscapes and serene waters, the Li River in Guilin is a picturesque escape. The dramatic scenery along the river is often described as 'the finest under heaven,' and it’s best explored by boat or on a scenic hike."

Zhangjiajie National Forest Park

"Known for its towering sandstone pillars, Zhangjiajie National Forest Park inspired the floating mountains in the movie Avatar. This UNESCO World Heritage Site offers breathtaking views of its unique rock formations, lush forests, and sky-high glass bridges."

Potala Palace

"Perched atop Marpo Ri Hill in Lhasa, the Potala Palace is an iconic symbol of Tibetan Buddhism and was the winter residence of the Dalai Lama. This majestic red and white palace is a masterpiece of Tibetan architecture and spirituality."

Terracotta Army

"Discovered in 1974, the Terracotta Army is an extraordinary archaeological find near Xi'an. This vast army of life-sized soldiers, horses, and chariots was buried with China's first emperor, Qin Shi Huang, to protect him in the afterlife."

West Lake

"West Lake in Hangzhou is a tranquil and scenic retreat surrounded by pagodas, temples, and gardens. With its calm waters and misty hills, it has been a source of inspiration for poets and artists for centuries."

Yu Garden

"Located in the bustling city of Shanghai, Yu Garden is a traditional Chinese garden offering serene ponds, rockeries, and pavilions. This beautiful oasis of tranquility stands in contrast to the modern skyscrapers of Shanghai."

Giant Panda Research Base

"Chengdu is home to the Giant Panda Research Base, where visitors can observe China's beloved national animal in its natural habitat. The center is dedicated to the conservation and breeding of these endangered creatures."

Tiger Leaping Gorge

"Tiger Leaping Gorge in Yunnan Province is one of the deepest gorges in the world, offering dramatic landscapes of jagged cliffs and the rushing Jinsha River below. It's a popular trekking destination for adventure enthusiasts."
